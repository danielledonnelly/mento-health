Soda Bottle Brain

A poem about mento health

My brain is a big bottle of soda.

It seems deceptively sweet and bubbly at first.

But it can be unhealthy and highly reactive.

When I get shaken up, my mind does too.

Little thoughts suddenly start to bubble to the surface.

⚪

Each and every thing that I worry about is just a mento.

A simple little candy. Nothing all that remarkable on its own.

But when I'm worried about everything all at once...

It feels like I've stuffed as many mentos as I can into my soda bottle brain.

I burst. It's messy. It's gross. I think so, at least.

⚪

I feel out of control. My thoughts are everywhere.

My worries quickly start to leak out of my rambling lips.

And there's nothing I can do.

There's no stopping an eruption once it starts.

You just have to wait it out and hope it ends soon.

⚪

I still think I'm lucky, all things considered.

I don't explode that often, only on rare occasions.

And I've gotten better at dealing with the aftermath.

I used to resent myself after every little spill.

But not anymore.

⚪

I don't think I'll ever be able to stop the mentos from piling up.

On those unfortunate instances when everything goes wrong at once...

I can't stop myself from bursting.

But that's okay. Because once the eruption is over, I can wash it all away.

In the end, I know I can always screw the cap back on my soda bottle brain.
